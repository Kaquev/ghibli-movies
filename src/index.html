Fun facts

In April 1990, Hayao Miyazaki and four other contributors established the Totoro no Furusato (Totoro's Home) Foundation with the aim of preserving the forested areas of the Sayama Hills by taking care of them, and preventing construction on these lands. "Kimitachi wa Dō Ikiru", the latest release from Studio Ghibli, had its premiere on July 14th 2023 without any prior promotion. The only thing that had been revealed was an image of a heron, which was used to design the movie poster. Miyazaki met Isao Takahata at the age of 22, when Isao was 27. Both of them were nature enthusiasts. In 1987, they produced a documentary about a river named "Yanagawa" and the local community's endeavors to conserve the natural beauty of the region. They later became the founders of Studio Ghibli. The book "Hayao Miyazaki Image Board" contains two stories in which the main character is "Totoro," although during those years, his name was "Tokorozawa no Obake". In an interview, Hayao Miyazaki revealed that the "Stink Spirit" in "Spirited Away" is a reference to a river he helped clean up in Tokorozawa. Hayao has admitted that the character "Dola" is inspired by his mother's strong temperament. "Mei to Nekubasu" (2002) is the only work by Hayao Miyazaki to have a sequel, exclusively showcased at the Ghibli Museum. Toshio Suzuki met Hayao Miyazaki in 1979 during the production of "Lupin III: The Castle of Cagliostro." This initial meeting sparked a friendship, and Suzuki was subsequently invited to become part of Studio Ghibli's staff in 1985.
